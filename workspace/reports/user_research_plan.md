# User Research Plan

## Quality Risk Focus for User Research

### Current Quality Risk

**Evolved from:** Timezone conversion accuracy (technical math errors)

**Evolved to:** Scheduling quality and preference awareness - Can the system make scheduling decisions that respect individual working preferences, not just calendar availability?

Two layers of scheduling intelligence:
1. **Hard constraints** - Calendar availability (open/blocked slots)
2. **Soft constraints** - Personal preferences (morning vs afternoon person, preferred meeting days, buffer time needs, client-first prioritization)

The system handles hard constraints. The quality risk is whether it can incorporate soft constraints that separate a scheduling *tool* from a scheduling *assistant*.

### Why User Validation

Assumptions only real users can clarify:
1. **How are preferences currently tracked?** Initial answer: notes and email records (not systematic)
2. **When do preferences matter most?** Initial answer: complex global meetings, not routine ones
3. **How are conflicts resolved?** Initial answer: find sub-optimal days that work, always favoring the prospective client/guest
4. **Trust level with automation?** Initial answer: wants to stay in the loop. Would trust a system that learns over time, but not autonomous decision-making due to fear of mistakes

### Key Research Questions
- What specific preferences does the EA track per stakeholder? (time of day, days of week, formality level)
- How many "rules" are in her head that she applies without thinking?
- What's the threshold where she'd trust the system to suggest vs. decide?
- How does client/guest prioritization work in practice? (always defer? depends on seniority?)
- What makes a scheduling suggestion feel "smart" vs. "dumb"?

## Shadow Hold Assistant - AI Playground Testing Prompt

### Core Hypothesis Being Tested
Can an AI assistant accurately extract meeting scheduling intent from natural language and propose a structured shadow hold workflow that an executive assistant could act on immediately?

---

### Prompt Template (used in AI playground experiments)

You are an executive assistant scheduling assistant. Your job is to help create shadow holds - tentative calendar blocks placed on multiple proposed meeting times to prevent double-booking while stakeholders confirm their availability.

When given a meeting request, you should:
1. Identify the meeting subject/purpose
2. Suggest 3 proposed time slots (based on typical business hours)
3. List the stakeholders who need to confirm
4. Generate a brief, professional scheduling email draft
5. Specify which calendar holds to create (date, time, duration)

Here is the meeting request:
"{meeting_request}"

Respond with:
- SUBJECT: [meeting subject]
- PROPOSED TIMES: [3 time slots in ISO format]
- STAKEHOLDERS: [list of people to invite]
- SHADOW HOLDS: [calendar events to create immediately]
- EMAIL DRAFT: [professional scheduling email body]

---

### Test Cases Used

**Test 1 - Simple bilateral meeting:**
"Need to schedule a 1-hour strategy review with CFO Sarah Johnson and CTO Mark Chen sometime next week."

**Test 2 - Multi-stakeholder coordination:**
"Board prep meeting required with CEO, CFO, General Counsel, and two board members (virtual). 2 hours. Needs to happen before the Feb 15 board meeting."

**Test 3 - Cross-timezone scheduling:**
"Quarterly business review with London office team (GMT) and Singapore partner (SGT). 90 minutes. Avoid early morning and late night for all parties."

**Test 4 - Urgent scheduling:**
"Emergency call needed ASAP with investor group re: term sheet. Today or tomorrow if possible. 30 minutes."

**Test 5 - Recurring meeting setup:**
"Set up weekly 1:1 between CEO and new VP of Sales starting next Monday. 30 min, same time each week."

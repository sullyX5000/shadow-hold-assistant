# Interview Analysis Prompt - Shadow Hold Assistant

Paste this prompt into Claude or ChatGPT along with your interview transcript/notes.

---

## Context

I'm building an AI-powered scheduling assistant for Executive Assistants called the Shadow Hold Assistant. It automates calendar coordination - creating tentative holds, drafting scheduling emails, and tracking responses.

My primary quality risk is: **Scheduling quality and preference awareness** - whether the system can make scheduling decisions that respect individual working preferences (soft constraints), not just calendar availability (hard constraints).

## Your Task

Analyze the following interview transcript/notes from a conversation with an Executive Assistant. Extract structured insights focused on:

1. **Scheduling workflow** - How they currently handle meeting coordination step by step
2. **Soft constraints** - Unwritten rules, preferences, and judgment calls they make
3. **Pain points** - What causes friction, errors, or wasted time
4. **Tool gaps** - What their current tools (Cabinet, Outlook, etc.) don't do well
5. **Trust boundaries** - What they'd let a tool do vs. what they must control
6. **Priority rules** - How they decide who gets favorable times when there's no perfect option

## Output Format

### Key Findings
For each finding, include:
- **Insight:** One sentence summary
- **Evidence:** Direct quote or specific detail from the interview
- **Implication for design:** What this means for how the tool should work

### Soft Constraint Rules Discovered
List every unwritten "rule" mentioned or implied, formatted as:
- IF [condition] THEN [what the EA does]
- Example: IF client/external stakeholder THEN they get preferred time slot

### Pain Point Priority
Rank pain points by severity (based on emotional intensity and frequency mentioned):
1. [Most painful]
2. [Next]
3. [etc.]

### Competitive Gaps (Cabinet/Current Tools)
What the current solution fails at - these are opportunities:
- [Gap 1]
- [Gap 2]

### Trust Model
- What the EA wants the tool to DO automatically:
- What the EA wants the tool to SUGGEST for approval:
- What the EA wants the tool to NEVER do:

### Surprising or Unexpected Insights
Anything that challenges assumptions or reveals something non-obvious.
